Did you know?

The Great Wall of China, a testament to human engineering and perseverance, stretches over 13,000 miles and is one of the most iconic structures in the world. Built over several dynasties, primarily during the Ming Dynasty, the wall served as a formidable defense against invasions and raids. Contrary to popular belief, the Great Wall is not a single continuous wall but a series of walls and fortifications strategically constructed along the northern borders of China. The labor force behind this colossal undertaking included soldiers, peasants, and prisoners, making it a massive collaborative effort.
